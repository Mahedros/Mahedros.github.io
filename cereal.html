Sometimes, the late-night brain allows a cursed thought to sneak in.Tonight's cursed thought: "I have a bunch of leftover diced onions. What if I put them in my breakfast cereal?"

— Mahedros (@mahedros.bsky.social) September 20, 2025 at 12:25 AM

So I put the onions in my cereal. Having done so there was really only one thing left to do:

Make more weird cereal and set up a totally rigorous, highly scientific ranking of all my weird creations. All testing is done with raisin bran crunch, with milk, and with no cooking unless specified.

Additional details are available by clicking on an entry. Some entries will need to be revisited before I can remember them well enough to provide details.

Most recent update:
April 19, 2026: Added Cheez-Its, Revisited Chives and Olive Oil

  1. Old Bay

    October 26, 2025
    Look, if you want to call me biased because I'm from Maryland, I can't stop you. But this is genuinely good and everyone should try it.

  2. Garam Masala

    November 22, 2025
    Not a huge surprise, but this is excellent. Garam masala is just really good, and that doesn't change just because you put in cereal. If you're not a Maryland cult member maybe this is the best one.

  3. Curry Powder

    February 16, 2026
    If what I said about garam masala was not enough, this is just reinforcement of that idea. The spices of indian cuisine are good. There's not much else to say.

  4. Marjoram

    October 25, 2025
    This needs revisiting. It was a while ago and I remember little about it.

  5. Angostura Aromatic Bitters

    February 28, 2026
    First things first, it tastes good. It wouldn't surprise me if this wasn't everyone's thing, but it works for me. I'll also have to admit to feeling a bit dumb here, in that I thought to myself "whoa, I can really smell this in each spoonful" before remembering that they're "Aromatic" bitters. Going to attribute that to just not being fully awake in the morning so I can feel better about myself.

  6. Orgeat

    December 13, 2025
    Sweet cereals are a thing. Cereals with almonds in them are a thing. Sugar, raisins, nuts, and granola is basically just trail mix. What a surprise, this is pretty good.

  7. Ginger

    December 20, 2025
    This needs revisiting. I remember little about it.

  8. Garlic Powder

    November 21, 2025
    This needs revisiting. It was a while ago and I remember little about it.

  9. Grenadine

    January 31, 2026
    After the success of orgeat, a different sweet syrup seemed like a reasonable thing to try. Pomegranate doesn't work as well as almond, but all things considered this was still pretty good.

  10. Chives

    November 9, 2025 - Revisited April 18, 2026
    Upon revisit, this has been shifted up slightly to be above Oyster Sauce. I incorrectly remembered it being pretty flavorless when I did it the first time, but I may have just not used enough. Subtle and pleasant, but be advised that you may need a bit of trial and error to get the right amount, and with it floating at the top of the bowl you may need to add some more in throughout the bowl to maintain the flavor.

  11. Olive Oil

    December 5, 2025 - Revisited April 19, 2026
    The mouthfeel here is the big obstacle. It's weird, but if you can get past that it's actually not a bad flavor. I'm not sure why I had this ranked as low as I did before. It's not good enough to break into the ranks of the truly good additions, but I can't deny it does add enough that is both tasty and unique to be above the "this tastes of nothing" group that makes up most of the middle of the list.

  12. Oyster Sauce

    April 5, 2026
    When doing fish sauce, the flavor was overpowering and I wondered if a smaller amount would have worked better. The thickness of the oyster sauce made getting a large amount out harder, which appears to have provided the answer. This worked much better.

  13. Salted Peanuts

    December 11, 2025
    Not a huge impact on flavor, but it fits in well from a textural perspective. Crunchy peanuts don't feel out of place with crunchy cereal, and the light salting isn't unwelcome.

  14. Fish Sauce

    April 4, 2026
    A lot of the other things in this part of the list don't have a particularly strong taste. Their impact on the cereal is subtle, and may be more textural than flavor. This is not the case for fish sauce. If you're weird enough to do this, you will not taste cereal. You will only taste fish sauce. Is it terrible? No. We've done far worse, especially when it comes to savory flavors. Is it good? Also no. Maybe this could work if I put less in, but that can wait. We have other ingredients to try.

  15. Yellow Onion

    September 21, 2025
    Here it is, the one that started it all. As bad as raw onion in cereal sounds, this really had less of an impact on things than I expected. The impact of onion is more on texture than flavor, and that impact isn't particularly unpleasant.

  16. Green Bell Pepper

    February 15, 2026
    Like with onion, this has very little impact on flavor. There's a textural change, and the spoonfuls that included bits of pepper tasted a bit more watery than those without.

  17. Cayenne Pepper

    November 15, 2025
    This needs revisiting. It was a while ago and I remember little about it.

  18. Cheez-Its

    April 12, 2026
    The flavor here isn't bad, but this ingredient commits a grave crime against all cereals. A good cereal, when in the presence of milk, retains its crunchy nature for a period of time so that one can finish the bowl before it turns into an unpleasant mush. Cheez-Its do not do this. They lose all structural integrity almost instantly, and therefore whether or not the bowl tastes good becomes almost irrelevant.

  22. Cilantro

    October 11, 2025
    This needs revisiting. It was a while ago and I remember little about it, but I suspect it shouldn't be this low.

  23. Pasta Sauce

    February 22, 2026
    From sight alone I knew this would be bad. Mixing pasta sauce with milk results in a very unappetizing shade of pink that is not great for one's appetite. Unfortunately, things don't get better from there. Tomato in cereal isn't good, and the texture of the chunkier parts of the sauce are everything you don't want in a bowl of cereal.

  24. MSG

    November 23, 2025
    The big lesson to take away from a lot of the entries at the bottom is that umami does not work well in cereal. Miso should have taught me that, but I gave it the benefit of the doubt and attributed that failure to my not putting any effort that goes into these. This really put the nail into the umami coffin. Don't do this.

  25. Worcestershire Sauce

    January 1, 2026
    After miso and MSG, I knew this would be bad. I learned my lesson, umami doesn't work. But a friend requested this for the list, and I am a man of the people. It was every bit as bad as I thought it would be.

  26. White Miso

    September 27, 2025
    It's really important to remember how little effort goes into the preparation here. Maybe this wouldn't be terrible if I bothered to try and really mix the miso into the milk. But we don't do that here. The problem is, when you just drop a spoon of miso into the cereal and give it a quick stir, what you end up with is chunks of miso. This means some spoonfuls will have no miso and taste like normal cereal, and others will taste like pure, undiluted miso. This is not good.

  27. Ketchup

    January 19, 2026
    Maybe this could be higher if I liked ketchup to begin with, but I can't imagine it would be that much higher. Even by ketchup standards I hated this.
